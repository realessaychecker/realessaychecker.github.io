]]> A lesson on effective writing – Main features of a good supporting sentence http://www.realessaychecker.com/a-lesson-on-effective-writing-main-features-of-a-good-supporting-sentence/ Mon, 25 Jan 2016 11:44:27 +0000 http://www.realessaychecker.com/?p=12305 What are the supporting details?

In the previous posts we introduced the main features of a topic sentence and we explained that you should take a position at the first sentence of the body paragraph. In this post we explain that you are required to support your stance with facts and evidences!

Fundamentally, the supporting details in a PTE or IELTS essay, helps the reader (or rather examiner!) to understand why the writer has claimed the topic sentence. In other words, supporting sentences argue why the writer (or rather the PTE or IELTS candidate) stated the topic sentence?

  • In your supporting sentence:
  • You can compare or contrast.
  • You can give examples.
  • You can elaborate on your reason for statement.
  • You can clarify your opinion by explaining it.

Remember that as supporting ideas you can write opposing sentences which disagree with a statement.

I am sure when you were studying tutorial materials of PTE or IELTS writing, you have come across with the expression of “Topic Sentence”. Basically what is it?! Today I am going to teach you about it!

Topic sentence is actually your main idea which is expressed clearly in the beginning of your paragraph. A good topic sentence will state what you are going to write in that paragraph. So, it contains the main point of the paragraph.

Topic sentence is generally followed by other sentences related to the main idea of the paragraph.

A good topic sentence should be descriptive and determine exactly what you are going to say. In other words, it should persuade the reader to follow your idea and paragraph.
